Our Flagship Product

SolarXite

A rooftop-first dual-axis solar tracking system designed to maximize energy output without the complexity of ground-mounted solutions.

The Problem

Traditional fixed-angle rooftop solar panels capture only a fraction of available sunlight. As the sun moves across the sky, panels lose efficiency—sometimes up to 30% during peak hours.

  • Fixed panels miss optimal sun angles
  • Ground trackers don't fit rooftops
  • Manual adjustment is impractical
  • High installation costs for existing solutions

Our Solution

SolarXite brings smart, automated dual-axis tracking to rooftops. Our compact design integrates seamlessly with existing installations, boosting output without major retrofits.

  • 25-40% more energy capture
  • Compact rooftop-friendly design
  • Automatic sun tracking
  • Cost-effective retrofit solution
Technology

How SolarXite Works

Our intelligent tracking system combines precision mechanics with smart algorithms.

1

Sense

Light sensors and algorithms detect the sun's position in real-time throughout the day.

2

Calculate

The control unit computes optimal panel angles for maximum light capture.

3

Adjust

Precision stepper motors smoothly rotate panels on both axes for perfect alignment.

Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Fixed panels are simpler but leave significant energy on the table. As the sun moves across the sky, fixed panels lose up to 30% of potential output because they can't maintain the optimal 90° angle to sunlight. SolarXite's dual-axis tracking keeps panels perpendicular to the sun throughout the day, capturing 25-40% more energy from the same panels. For rooftop installations where space is limited, maximizing output per panel is crucial.

Adding more panels requires more rooftop space—which many urban buildings simply don't have. SolarXite lets you extract maximum value from the panels you already have, within your existing footprint. Additionally, tracking provides better energy distribution throughout the day, generating more power during morning and evening hours when fixed systems produce less. This can be valuable for time-of-use electricity pricing.

SolarXite uses industrial-grade stepper motors designed for years of continuous operation. The tracking movements are slow and controlled, minimizing wear. The system includes fail-safe positioning—if any component fails, panels default to a fixed optimal angle rather than stopping production entirely. We're also designing for modular replacement, so individual components can be swapped without removing the entire system.

SolarXite is designed for flat and low-slope rooftops commonly found in commercial buildings, warehouses, and many residential properties. The compact design minimizes wind load and structural requirements. However, steep-pitched roofs, shaded areas, or rooftops with significant obstructions may not be ideal candidates. We recommend a site assessment to determine suitability for your specific installation.

The tracking system's power consumption is minimal—typically less than 1% of the additional energy gained from tracking. Our stepper motors are high-efficiency models that only activate during position adjustments, not continuously. The net energy gain from tracking far exceeds the system's operational power requirements.
